Mounting and Connecting the Commutator#

Mounting#

The commutator can be mounted to a standard 1” 80/20 aluminum extrusion using t-nuts or a standard mechanical breadboard. For custom mounting solutions, the dimensions for the mounting holes (1/4”-20 or M6) are indicated in the below schematic:

For more clarity, access the 3D model which can be downloaded here or in the Mechanical section of our Developer Guide which contains more information about the enclosure and how to view such a file.

If the commutator is used for an animal behavior experiment, mount the commutator such that the animal can traverse the behavior setup’s full area given the length of your tether.

Connecting#

There are three electrical interconnects on the commutator:

  1. Micro-USB Connector:

    2. The commutator receives power and communicates to another device (probably a computer) with serial communication (USB/UART) through this interconnect

  2. Top SMA Connector:

    3. The commutator’s stator connects to the stationary data acquisition device (DAQ) through this interconnect

  3. Bottom SMA Connector:

    4. The commutator’s rotor connects to the freely moving animal headstage through this interconnect

Attention

  • Ensure that the Micro-USB carries digital signals as well as power

  • Source your coaxial tether from a reputable vendor such as Digikey, Mouser, or Open Ephys
